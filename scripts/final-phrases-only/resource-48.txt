SECTION 3: SPECIFIC EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS
================================================================================

UNCONSCIOUS PASSENGER:
1. Pull over safely immediately
2. Check if breathing - look for chest movement
3. Call 911 immediately
4. If not breathing: Begin CPR if trained (not required)
5. Do NOT leave patient alone
6. Wait for paramedics

CHEST PAIN (POSSIBLE HEART ATTACK):
1. Pull over safely immediately
2. Call 911 - state "possible heart attack"
3. Keep patient calm and still
4. Ask if they have medication (nitroglycerin) - help them take it
5. Do NOT drive to hospital - wait for ambulance
6. Monitor breathing until help arrives

DIFFICULTY BREATHING:
1. Pull over safely immediately
2. Lower windows for fresh air
3. Ask about inhaler/EpiPen - help them use it
4. Call 911 if severe or not improving
5. Keep patient sitting upright
6. Stay calm to help them stay calm

SEIZURE:
1. Pull over safely immediately
2. Clear area around person (move sharp objects)
3. Do NOT restrain them or put anything in their mouth
4. Time the seizure (call 911 if longer than 5 minutes)
5. Turn person on side after seizure stops
6. Stay with them until fully conscious

SEVERE BLEEDING:
1. Pull over safely immediately
2. Call 911
3. Apply direct pressure with clean cloth
4. Do NOT remove cloth if blood soaks through - add more on top
5. Elevate injured area above heart if possible
6. Keep patient lying down

================================================================================
SECTION 4: AFTER THE EMERGENCY
================================================================================

DOCUMENTATION:
1. File detailed platform report with timeline
2. Note: time emergency started, 911 call time, ambulance arrival
3. Hospital name where patient was taken
4. Any witnesses who can provide statement
5. Your emotional state is valid - consider counseling support

LEGAL PROTECTION:
- Good Samaritan laws protect you when helping in emergency
- You are NOT required to provide medical care beyond calling 911
- Platform insurance should cover any liability during emergency
- Document everything for insurance purposes

EMOTIONAL RECOVERY:
- Medical emergencies are traumatic for drivers too
- Platform may offer counseling resources
- Take time off if needed - this is legitimate trauma
- Talk to someone about what you experienced

MEDICAL DISCLAIMER
================================================================================

This resource provides basic emergency response phrases and protocols for
rideshare drivers. It is NOT a substitute for formal first aid/CPR training.

RECOMMENDED: Take a basic first aid and CPR course through Red Cross or similar
organization. Cost: $40-90. Time: 4-6 hours. Could save a life.

In any medical emergency, your PRIMARY responsibility is to call 911 and
provide basic comfort until professional help arrives. You are NOT expected
or required to provide medical treatment beyond this.
