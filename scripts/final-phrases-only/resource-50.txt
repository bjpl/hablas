SECTION 3: THREAT RESPONSE PROTOCOLS BY SEVERITY
================================================================================

LEVEL 1: UNCOMFORTABLE BUT NOT THREATENING
(Inappropriate comments, persistent personal questions, minor rudeness)
- Set verbal boundary: "Please keep conversation professional"
- Document behavior in trip notes
- Rate low and report after trip completion
- Block passenger from future trips

LEVEL 2: CONCERNING BEHAVIOR
(Aggressive tone, invasion of personal space, refusing reasonable requests)
- State boundary clearly: "I need you to [specific action]"
- Consider ending trip early
- Document immediately
- Report to platform with detailed description
- If behavior escalates â†’ move to Level 3

LEVEL 3: ACTIVE THREAT
(Threats of violence, displaying weapon, physical aggression)
ðŸš¨ IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. Pull over in public area with witnesses (gas station, store parking lot)
2. Use phrase #8: "Get out of the car now"
3. Use phrase #7: "I'm calling the police" (actually call 911)
4. If safe: exit vehicle and move away
5. If passenger won't exit: use phrase #9 to get public attention
6. Do NOT drive to isolated area - stay where there are people
7. Call 911 and stay on line with dispatcher

LEVEL 4: ACTIVE ASSAULT
(Physical contact, assault, being held against will)
ðŸš¨ LIFE-SAVING ACTIONS:
1. DRIVE TO POLICE STATION if you know location
2. OR drive to busy public area and activate panic response
3. Use horn repeatedly to get attention (SOS: 3 short, 3 long, 3 short)
4. If vehicle stopped: exit immediately and run toward people/buildings
5. SHOUT phrase #9 repeatedly: "I need help, I'm in danger"
6. Make as much noise as possible - activate car alarm, keep shouting
7. Your goal: PUBLIC ATTENTION and DISTANCE from attacker

SECTION 4: WEAPON SITUATIONS - ARMED PASSENGER PROTOCOL
================================================================================

ðŸš¨ IF YOU SEE A WEAPON OR WEAPON IS MENTIONED ðŸš¨

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. Stay calm - do not make sudden movements
2. Comply with demands if weapon is pointed at you
3. Your life > your property > the vehicle > any money
4. Try to safely end trip in public location
5. Memorize details: weapon type, physical description, clothing
6. Call 911 the INSTANT it is safe to do so
7. Drive to police station if possible and safe

DURING ARMED INCIDENT:
- Do NOT argue or resist
- Do NOT try to disarm unless immediate life threat
- Give them what they want (money, phone, etc.)
- Pay attention to details for police report
- Try to stay calm - fear can escalate situation

AFTER ARMED INCIDENT:
1. Call 911 immediately
2. Drive to police station or wait for police at safe location
3. Do NOT touch anything in vehicle (preserve evidence)
4. Write down everything you remember while fresh
5. Cooperate fully with police investigation
6. Contact platform immediately
7. Seek counseling - this is traumatic

SECTION 5: SEXUAL HARASSMENT & ASSAULT PROTOCOLS
================================================================================

ðŸš¨ ZERO TOLERANCE: Sexual harassment and assault are NEVER acceptable. You have
ABSOLUTE RIGHT to end trip immediately and report to police.

DEFINING THE BEHAVIOR:
- Unwanted sexual comments or advances
- Requests for personal contact info
- Inappropriate touching (any unwanted physical contact)
- Exposure of private body parts
- Sexual assault (forced sexual contact)

IMMEDIATE RESPONSE:
1. State boundary: "That behavior is inappropriate and unacceptable"
2. Pull over in public location immediately
3. Use phrase #8: "Get out of the car now"
4. If they refuse or escalate: Call 911
5. Do NOT continue trip under any circumstances

REPORTING PROCESS:
1. Call 911 for police report (creates legal record)
2. Report to platform immediately (creates company record)
3. Request permanent ban of passenger
4. Consider contacting lawyer (some attorneys offer free consultation)
5. Seek counseling support (platform may provide resources)

EVIDENCE PRESERVATION:
- Do NOT clean or detail vehicle until police inspect
- Save all platform messages
- Write detailed timeline while memory is fresh
- Request dashcam footage if available
- List any witnesses (other drivers, pedestrians, etc.)

YOUR RIGHTS:
- You have right to prosecute criminally
- You have right to civil lawsuit for damages
- Platform cannot retaliate against you for reporting
- You may qualify for victim compensation funds
- Support organizations can help navigate legal process
